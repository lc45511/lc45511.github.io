Activity 1: Complete the following tutorials

The first activity involved completing the LearnFree: Internet Safety tutorial and the TutorialsPoint: Internet Security tutorial. These learning activities focused on protecting personal information, devices, and online accounts while using the internet. The LearnFree tutorial covered a wide range of topics, including creating strong passwords, understanding browser security features, avoiding spam and phishing scams, preventing malware infections, practicing safe online shopping, managing social media privacy settings, improving Wi-Fi security, and recognizing browser tracking. The TutorialsPoint: Internet Security tutorial expanded on these concepts by introducing internet security protocols such as Internet Security Protocol (IPSec) and Secure Socket Layer (SSL), which help protect online communications through authentication, privacy, and data integrity. It also discussed common internet security threats, including malware, ransomware, spam, hacking, and phishing attacks.

Through these activities, I learned how important it is to remain aware of potential security risks while using the internet. One of the most valuable lessons was gaining a deeper understanding of phishing attempts and online scams, which connected to Assignment 3, where I learned similar concepts from GCF Global: Email Basics. Some of the overlap included warning signs such as suspicious sender addresses, poor grammar, urgent requests for personal information, unexpected attachments, and links that redirect users to fraudulent websites. Beyond that, I also learned how security technologies such as SSL help protect sensitive information during online transactions and communications. In addition, the tutorials reinforced the importance of strong passwords, software updates, secure Wi-Fi networks, and privacy settings as part of a comprehensive approach to online safety.

These concepts can be applied in accounting, marketing, and general business environments where sensitive information is frequently stored and transmitted online. In accounting, recognizing phishing attempts and protecting login credentials can help prevent unauthorized access to financial records and client information. In marketing, maintaining secure websites and protecting customer data helps build trust and credibility with consumers. More generally, understanding internet safety and security practices can help businesses reduce cybersecurity risks, protect confidential information, and maintain reliable digital operations. As technology continues to play a larger role in both personal and professional lives, developing strong internet security habits will remain an essential skill in any career.

Activity 2: Research password managers and multi-factor authentication

For this activity, I researched password managers and multi-factor authentication (MFA) and learned how both can improve online security. Password managers are tools that securely store, generate, and autofill passwords, allowing users to maintain strong, unique passwords for multiple accounts without having to remember each one. Instead of reusing the same password across multiple websites, a password manager can create complex passwords that are more difficult for cybercriminals to guess or crack. I also learned that password managers help reduce "password fatigue," which happens when users struggle to remember several login credentials and may resort to using weaker or repeated passwords. By storing credentials in an encrypted vault, password managers make it easier to manage online accounts while improving overall security. This is especially important as more personal, educational, and professional activities move online and require secure account access.

I also learned that password managers come in several forms, including browser-based, local, cloud-based, enterprise, and hardware solutions. I currently use browser-based password managers, which are built into browsers such as Chrome and Safari, and I found it interesting to learn more about how they help protect accounts while making logins more convenient. However, I also learned that some browser-based password managers may not offer the same level of security and functionality as dedicated password management applications, particularly when it comes to features such as secure cross-device syncing and end-to-end encryption. While they provide convenience and stronger security, they can still be vulnerable if a device becomes infected with malware or if a master password is compromised. Multi-factor authentication adds an extra layer of protection by requiring a second form of verification, such as a code sent to a phone, an authentication app, or a security key. This means that even if a password is stolen through a phishing scam or data breach, an attacker is less likely to gain access to the account. One example I have used recently comes from Harper College itself. To log in to the payment portal, users must enter an authenticator code, an example of mobile phone-based authentication, which helps secure payment methods and personal information. This activity connected well with the internet safety tutorials, especially the topics of strong passwords, phishing prevention, and protecting personal information online. Using a password manager together with MFA is considered one of the most effective ways to secure online accounts.

These practices are especially important in accounting, marketing, and other business fields where professionals regularly handle sensitive information. In accounting, strong account security helps protect financial records, tax documents, payroll information, and client data from unauthorized access or cyberattacks. In marketing, employees often manage social media accounts, advertising platforms, websites, and customer databases, all of which could be vulnerable if passwords are weak or compromised. More generally, businesses rely on online systems for communication, file sharing, banking, project management, and customer relationship management. Understanding how to use password managers and multi-factor authentication can help reduce security risks, prevent data breaches, and protect both company and customer information. As organizations continue to depend on digital technology, cybersecurity awareness and good password practices will remain valuable skills in any professional career.
