Activity 1: Search Engines Tutorials

The first activity was completing four different tutorials: LearnFree: Search Better, TutorialsPoint: Search Engine, LearnFree: ChatGPT for Beginners, and LearnFree: A Beginner's Guide to SEO Basics. These tutorials allowed me to explore tips for using search engines more effectively, gain a stronger understanding of what search engines are and how they work, learn practical ways to use ChatGPT, and recognize the importance of SEO.

LearnFree: Search Better focused primarily on Google, the most widely used search engine. It provided several strategies to keep in mind while searching and explained how to better understand and evaluate search results. TutorialsPoint: Search Engine offered a broader perspective on search engines as a whole. This learning activity explained the purpose of search engines and how they help users locate information on the internet. The tutorial covered the major components of a search engine, including web crawlers, indexes, search algorithms, and user interfaces. It also described the three stages of how search engines work: web crawling, web indexing, and searching and ranking. In addition, the tutorial introduced the concept of Search Engine Optimization (SEO), which was explored further in LearnFree: A Beginner's Guide to SEO Basics. It also provided brief descriptions of several popular search engines. LearnFree: ChatGPT for Beginners was a series of videos covering different ways to use ChatGPT. The videos began with a general overview of its use cases and advantages before focusing on specifics, such as creating an account, writing prompts, attaching files, and getting started with the platform. The final tutorial, LearnFree: A Beginner's Guide to SEO Basics, was shorter but emphasized important concepts such as focusing on user intent instead of stuffing content with keywords, the importance of content quality and structure, and maintaining consistency.

There are various ways these concepts can be applied. Personally, I use advanced search features frequently when working on research papers because they allow me to narrow results to the correct time period and filter topics enough to make them useful for my purposes, which is a skill that can be valuable in any career. Translation features can also be useful in business settings when quick clarification is needed while communicating with someone who speaks another language. I previously took an AI marketing class here at Harper College that helped me connect the different ways AI tools like ChatGPT can refine ideas, generate outlines, and improve efficiency. SEO, in particular, is crucial in marketing because it helps businesses improve visibility, increase engagement, and connect with their intended audience. Understanding how these tools work will help me conduct better research, communicate more effectively, and use digital technologies more strategically in my future career.

Activity 2: Compare Search Engines

The second activity involved searching for the term "web search engine" using Google, Bing, and Yahoo, then comparing the results from each platform. Ask was originally part of the assignment, but it was discontinued as of May 1, 2026. Although all three search engines returned relevant information about what a web search engine is and how it functions, there were noticeable differences in their interfaces and the way results were presented. Google provided a very clean and simple layout with minimal distractions, making it easy to scan through results quickly. Bing offered a more visually appealing interface with additional features such as its Video Creator and Image Creator tools, while Yahoo presented a combination of search results and integrated content, including news, finance, and sports sections on its homepage. Bing also features a news section, but it felt less obtrusive. Yahoo and Bing placed sponsored results at the top of the page, while Google did not in this particular search. All three offered similar filters, such as Images, Videos, Maps, and News, and each included some form of an AI-powered answer engine. Bing integrates Copilot, Google uses Gemini, and Yahoo uses Yahoo! Scout.

Out of the three, I felt that Google had the best user interface because of its simplicity, speed, and ease of navigation. It was the easiest to use and made finding information efficient without overwhelming the user with extra content. I also found that Google provided the most useful results, as the information appeared highly relevant to my search query and included a variety of credible sources. Bing was a close second because of its organized layout and useful visual elements. In comparison, Yahoo had the most distractions and featured more intrusive advertisements.

Using Google to search for "search engine market share," I found that Google itself has the highest market share by a significant margin. Recent statistics showed that Google holds approximately 90% of the global search engine market, making it the dominant search engine worldwide. Bing ranked second with roughly 5% of the market, followed by Yahoo with only 1.4%.

I was not surprised by these results because Google is the search engine I use most often due to its speed, familiarity, and the relevance of its results. It was interesting, however, to see just how large the gap is between Google and its competitors. Understanding search engine market share is particularly important in marketing because businesses often focus their SEO efforts on the platforms that provide the greatest visibility and reach. At the same time, these findings highlighted that other search engines still have a small, dedicated user base and may be worth considering depending on the target audience. This activity helped me recognize that not all search engines function in exactly the same way, even when given identical search terms. Understanding these differences can be useful in accounting, marketing, and general business, as different search engines may surface different perspectives, competitors, trends, or resources that support research and decision-making. In any future career, knowing how to evaluate and choose the most effective search tools will help me conduct more thorough research, gather reliable information, and make more informed decisions.
