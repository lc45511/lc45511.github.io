Activity 1: Complete the following tutorials (email)

The email activity involved completing several tutorials related to email communication and management, including Learn Free: Gmail Basics, GCF Global: Email Basics, and TutorialsPoint: Email Basics. These tutorials provided an overview of what email is, how it functions, and why it remains one of the most widely used methods of communication in both personal and professional settings. They covered concepts such as composing messages, using the To, CC, and BCC fields appropriately, attaching files, organizing inboxes, and maintaining proper email etiquette. Learn Free: Gmail Basics focused specifically on Gmail, highlighting its benefits and unique features, such as its integration with the Google ecosystem through tools like Google Docs and YouTube, as well as its user-friendly design.

Through these activities, I learned that email is more than simply sending messages. It is a professional communication tool that requires attention to clarity, organization, and tone. I gained a better understanding of when to use CC and BCC, how email signatures contribute to professionalism, and the benefits and drawbacks of email communication. While email is fast, inexpensive, reliable, and allows users to communicate globally, it also comes with challenges. Spam, information overload, accidental misdirection, and delayed responses are all factors that can make email difficult to manage. From GCF Global: Email Basics, I took away a lot from the sections on spam and scams. I've personally encountered several instances of spam emails attempting to access my information while I was attending Elmhurst University. There were phishing scams that tried to imitate official school emails and trick students into entering their information into Google Forms, allowing scammers to gain access to our accounts and personal information.

These concepts can be applied in both accounting and marketing. In accounting, professional emails are essential when communicating with clients, supervisors, and coworkers, especially regarding financial documents, deadlines, and requests for information. In marketing, email serves as both an internal communication tool and an important channel for customer outreach and email marketing campaigns. More broadly, effective email communication improves collaboration, strengthens professional relationships, and helps maintain an organized record of conversations and decisions. Developing strong email habits now will help me communicate more confidently, professionally, and efficiently throughout my future career.

Activity 2: Send, receive, and respond to email (email)

The second email activity involved completing the LearnFree: Gmail: Sending Email tutorial and practicing the process of sending and replying to email messages. The email service provider I chose to use was Gmail. The tutorial walked through the steps of composing a new email, entering recipients, creating subject lines, formatting messages, attaching files, and sending emails. It also demonstrated how to read incoming messages, use the reply function to continue conversations, and organize communication within Gmail. Since I already had a Gmail account, I used it to send an email with the subject line "Test Email" and the message, "This is a test for my class. Please send a funny picture!" to my best friend. I made my best friend the primary recipient, CC'd another friend, and BCC'd my alternate account so I could practice using each field correctly. My friends replied with funny GIFs, and I completed the second part of the assignment by replying within the same email thread and sending my own funny GIF back.

Through this activity, I learned that effective email communication involves more than simply clicking the send button. Writing clear and concise messages, including descriptive subject lines, and responding in a timely manner all contribute to professional and efficient communication. I also became more aware of the importance of reviewing messages before sending them to avoid errors, especially when replying to others or attaching documents. The tutorial reinforced how email conversations can serve as an organized record of communication and help ensure that important information is documented.

These concepts can be applied to any career. Gmail in particular is the largest email provider in the world, and knowing how to use it effectively is an important workplace skill. Many organizations use Gmail through Google Workspace, making it essential to understand features such as organizing inboxes, managing email threads, using CC and BCC appropriately, attaching and sharing files through Google Drive, and integrating with tools like Google Docs, Google Calendar, and Google Meet. Being comfortable with Gmail can improve communication, increase productivity, and make collaboration with coworkers and clients more efficient. As I enter the professional world, having experience with Gmail will help me adapt more easily to workplace expectations and communicate with confidence.
